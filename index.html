Cat-scratch Disease

What is Cat-Scratch Disease?

Cat-scratch disease is a disease caused by the bacterium Bartonella henselae.
It is carried by cats, who transmit it through scratching and biting.
The incubation period for cat-scratch disease is
two weeks.

Bartonella henselae cells, stained with Warthin-Starry reagent

Method of Transmission

Cat-scratch disease is primarily transmitted through scratches and bites from cats.
However, fleas on cats can also carry B. henselae
and dogs have been known to rarely transmit the disease.

Images of Felis catus, the primary carriers of cat-scratch disease

Symptoms

After two weeks, the site of infection can begin to show pus-filled lesions, redness and inflammation.
Other symptoms include fever, headache, lack of appetite, and fatigue.
Symptoms last anywhere from two to four months, but most people make a full recovery afterwards.
